The 12 traditional fast food stocks we track reported a strong Q4. As a group, revenues beat analysts’ consensus estimates by 1.1%. With nicknames spanning Mickey D's in the U.S. to Makku in Japan, McDonald’s (NYSE:MCD) is a fast-food behemoth known for its convenience and broken ice cream machines. McDonald's reported revenues of $7.01 billion, up 9.7% year on year. This print exceeded analysts’ expectations by 2.6%. Overall, it was a very strong quarter for the company with an impressive beat of analysts’ same-store sales and revenue estimates. "McDonald's value leadership is working," said Chairman and CEO Chris Kempczinski. The stock is down 4.2% since reporting and currently trades at $309.53.
