Colgate-Palmolive’s modeled fair value has been trimmed slightly from US$97.26 to US$96.68 per share, a change of about US$0.58 that keeps the overall valuation view broadly intact while acknowledging updated inputs. That small adjustment sits against mixed Street commentary, where some analysts highlight the long term 2030 plan and others flag cost and valuation risks that have led to price targets moving both higher and lower. Read on to see how these shifting targets, recent news, and new guidance fit together and how to keep up with the evolving Colgate-Palmolive story. BofA and Goldman Sachs lifted price targets earlier in the year, with BofA moving to US$105 and Goldman Sachs to US$100, citing confidence in Colgate-Palmolive’s 2030 plan and company specific actions around product launches and growth initiatives. Goldman Sachs highlighted exposure to emerging markets and the company’s SGPP and 2030 plan as supportive for future growth, alongside expectations for less drag from the private label pet food exit. Wells Fargo, UBS, JPMorgan, Piper Sandler and others also raised targets in early 2026, reflecting refreshed views on sector positioning and company execution, while Deutsche Bank issued an upgrade in late March. Since late March, TD Cowen turned more cautious, downgrading Colgate-Palmolive and pointing to higher oil based input costs as a headwind for margins and valuation. More recently, BofA, RBC Capital, Piper Sandler, Wells Fargo and UBS all trimmed price targets, signaling increased attention to cost pressures and prior valuation levels after a period of target increases earlier in the year.
