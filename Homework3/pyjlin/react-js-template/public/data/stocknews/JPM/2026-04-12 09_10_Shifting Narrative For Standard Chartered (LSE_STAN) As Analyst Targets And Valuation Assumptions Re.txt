Standard Chartered’s fair value price target has been trimmed slightly from £19.25 to £18.96, with other Street targets now clustering from £18.30 up to £22.00. That small shift mirrors the current mix of analyst views, where upbeat earnings assumptions sit alongside questions about how much investors should be willing to pay and why some targets, such as £18.65 from £21.22, are being reset. JPMorgan has been setting some of the higher valuation markers for Standard Chartered, with a price target most recently at 2,200 GBp. This signals confidence in the bank’s ability to execute on its current plan. Citi has lifted its target twice, from 1,675 GBp to 1,775 GBp and then to 1,830 GBp, while keeping a Neutral stance. This shows that even more cautious voices see some support for the current valuation case. Morgan Stanley’s target reset from 2,122 GBp to 1,865 GBp still sits toward the upper half of the current range. This suggests its analysts continue to see room for value if the bank delivers on earnings expectations. The most recent move from Morgan Stanley, trimming the target from 2,122 GBp to 1,865 GBp, underlines that some prior optimism is being reassessed as analysts scrutinise what investors should reasonably pay. Citi’s Neutral rating, despite higher targets, points to questions around execution and growth visibility. Standard Chartered is reported to remain a prime broker to Infini Capital Management in a March 2025 SEC filing, after other banks, including JPMorgan and UBS, reportedly cut ties following a Hong Kong insider dealing probe. The bank is reported to be exploring a crypto prime brokerage within its SC Ventures unit, with early stage discussions focused on a structure aligned to Basel III capital rules and existing digital asset holdings such as Zodia Custody. Standard Chartered has announced a new share repurchase program of up to US$1,500m, following completion of a prior US$1,300m buyback that covered 62,222,365 shares, or 2.71% of the company. The board has authorized another buyback plan, announced a proposed final dividend of US$0.49 per share payable on 14 May 2026, and appointed Peter Burrill as interim Group CFO from 10 February 2026. Fair value trimmed from £19.25 to £18.96, reflecting updated model assumptions. Revenue growth set at 6.18% compared with the prior 6.02% outlook. Net profit margin revised to 26.06% from 25.85%. Future P/E moved down from 9.88x to 9.64x. Discount rate adjusted from 8.37% to 8.29%.
