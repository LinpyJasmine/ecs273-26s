Goldman Sachs raised its Argan price target by US$119 on March 27, tying its view to Argan’s exposure to power projects that can support AI and data center demand for firm generation capacity. Lake Street also lifted its Argan price target by US$50 on the same date, reflecting a more constructive stance on the company’s project pipeline and ability to execute on contracted work. JPMorgan upgraded Argan on March 27 after what it called a strong Q4 report, signaling increased confidence in management’s delivery on current projects and the outlook for future awards. Freedom Capital, in its initiation with a Hold rating and a US$425 price target, highlighted that Argan is positioned to participate in growing power demand tied to AI and data centers. Freedom Capital’s Hold stance and US$425 target suggest some caution around how much of the AI and data center power theme is already reflected in Argan’s valuation, especially relative to more upbeat price targets from Goldman Sachs and Lake Street.
