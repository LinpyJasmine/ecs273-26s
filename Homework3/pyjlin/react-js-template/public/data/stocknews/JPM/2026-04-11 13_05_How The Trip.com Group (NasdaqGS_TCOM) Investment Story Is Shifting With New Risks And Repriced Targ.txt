Barclays and JPMorgan both cut their Trip.com price targets to $75 from $90 but kept Overweight ratings. In their view, the long term story still supports a premium to current trading levels even after revising assumptions. Benchmark reduced its target to $72 from $82 and maintained a Buy rating after what it called a solid Q4 beat and in line Q1 outlook. The firm cited continued revenue growth expectations while accepting lower margins due to reinvestment. This may limit how quickly profitability metrics can improve. Rosen Law Firm filed a class action lawsuit on behalf of Trip.com Group investors who bought securities between April 30, 2024 and January 13, 2026, alleging that management understated regulatory risks tied to monopolistic business activities and issued materially misleading disclosures. The lawsuit claims that when details about the alleged regulatory risks became clearer, Trip.com investors incurred damages, and it seeks recovery under federal securities laws. Trip.com Group scheduled a board meeting for February 25, 2026 to approve and publish its Q4 and full year 2025 financial results, which will update investors on the company’s operating and financial profile. Separately, Baidu reported that its Ernie AI assistant surpassed 200 million monthly active users, offering broader context for investors following China’s large platform and tech ecosystems where Trip.com operates.
