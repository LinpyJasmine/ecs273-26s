EHang Holdings is back in focus after analysts trimmed their price target from US$17 to US$16, while also updating their longer term fair value estimate from US$20.12 to US$19.78 per share. This shift reflects recent research that balances confidence in the company’s long term potential with a more measured view on how quickly eVTOL deliveries and related revenue might build. BofA, through analyst Fiona Liang, kept a Buy rating on EHang even after revising its price target to US$16. This signals continued confidence in the shares at current levels. The bank cited Q4 results that came in ahead of its expectations, which it factors into its updated view on the company. BofA cut its 2026 and 2027 eVTOL volume sales forecasts by 24% and 27%, respectively. This points to a more cautious stance on how quickly deliveries might build. The move from a US$17 to US$16 price target suggests the firm now sees slightly less upside than before. This is tied to these lower volume assumptions and a more measured growth trajectory. EHang announced a dual-feature aerial performance at the Hefei sub-venue of the 2026 China Media Group Spring Festival Gala, combining passenger carrying EH216-S aircraft with a large scale drone show. A formation of 16 EH216-S pilotless human-carrying eVTOL aircraft flew simultaneously above the "Eye of Anhui" main stage in Hefei Luogang Park, which the company describes as its largest simultaneous public flight of pilotless eVTOL aircraft so far. EHang's subsidiary, EHang Egret, organized a drone light show with 22,580 GHOSTDRONE 4.0 UAVs, setting a new Guinness World Records title for the most multirotor or drones airborne simultaneously from a single computer. The Hefei performances highlighted EHang's cluster command and control system, algorithmic control and multi scenario adaptive flight capabilities within an urban low altitude setting at Luogang Park, where regular EH216-S trial operations have been running since March 2025 under an Air Operator Certificate.
